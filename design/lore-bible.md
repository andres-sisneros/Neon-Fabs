# Neon Fabs Lore Bible

## Core Feeling

Neon Fabs is about contract drifters, civic matter, sleeper crews, and machine-made scarcity. The world should feel cyberpunk, but not only chrome and violence: public assemblers, route debt, soft borders, and fragile communities should matter.

## Creative North Star

Players are not chosen heroes. They are small operators learning how to survive inside a logistics ecology where matter can be printed, but access is rationed by power, routes, markets, civic permissions, and trust.

## Influences To Use As Mood, Not Source Text

- Cyberpunk Red: street-level hustle, roles, cargo risk, security contracts, and city markets.
- The Diamond Age: matter compilers, artificial scarcity, civic order, and societies shaped by fabrication access.
- Citizen Sleeper / Citizen Sleeper 2: precarious crews, orbital-worker debt, faction favors, and survival through networks.
- Death Stranding: delivery as social infrastructure, dangerous corridors, shared route maintenance, and the relief of making a road safer.

## World Rules

- Fabs are constrained by power, feedstock, permissions, and maintenance.
- Markets are local because inventory is local.
- Routes remember labor. Clearing threats can stabilize a route for everyone.
- Melds are civic-memory sets, not normal inventory.
- Scarcity is political. The world can print abundance, but access is mediated.

## Open Questions

- What exactly are starter components in-world?
- Why does a meld add battery capacity?
- Who regulates fab ownership and rentals?
- Which city is most welcoming to a new operator, and which is most predatory?
