## High-Confidence Mechanics

- Setting: year 4024, humans return from Mars after Yellowstone covered Earth in silty soil.
- Core loop: players mine "things" from old Earth rather than raw materials.
- Mining runs in real time and continues while logged out.
- Players start with one free Starter mine.
- Early game is intentionally slow: a forum snippet says starting rate was about 30 buckets/hour and about 180 buckets per average find, or roughly one find per 6 hours.
- Mining output can be items or gold. The mine page has radio buttons for "mine gold" and "mine things."
- Mines show production in "buckets per hour."
- Equipment improves bucket rate.
- Starter mine reportedly had about 50 different things.
- There were nine mine types spread across five cities.
- Mine categories include Starter, Equipment, Vehicles, Weapons, Ships, Gadgets, Explosives, Music, Fish, Bugs, Spices, and likely others shown in screenshots.
- Equipment mine had 42 pieces of equipment.
- Vehicle mine had 24 vehicles used for trader/pirate/bounty-hunter route play.
- Gadgets provided temporary stat/ability boosts.
- Explosives provided instant one-time bursts of mining progress.
- Items had rarity tiers by color; yellow/common, blue/rarer, orange/rarest are mentioned by a player review.
- Some orange items remained undiscovered for long stretches.

## Collection And Market

- The main progression goal was creating "melds" from item combinations.
- Meld count corresponded to player level.
- Higher meld counts unlocked additional professions.
- Items could be bought/sold automatically against highest bids/lowest listings.
- Players set market prices.
- Items used in more melds or rarer finds tended to be worth more.
- Mines themselves could be bought from other players for gold.
- New mines entered the economy through real-money credit purchases.
- A 2013 review says the cheapest starter mine cost 75 credits and the most expensive mine cost 675 credits.
- The same review says starter mines could cost around 6000g in the player market at one point.
- A forum snippet says Bromo starter mines were about 1800g in late 2010, suggesting server/economy variation.

## Travel, PvP, And Professions

- Goods found or bought in other cities needed to be transported home to make melds.
- Cities had local item supply; goods were more valuable away from their origin city.
- Moving goods exposed them to route PvP.
- Land routes used vehicles; sea routes used ships.
- Professions were transport-specific and mutually limiting: while moving goods by land, a player could not simultaneously move by sea; while hunting pirates at sea, they could not guard land routes.
- Trader: uses vehicles to move goods city-to-city for profit.
- Merchant: ship-based trader, can reach cities outside the main island and carry more cargo.
- Highwayman: land-route raider.
- Pirate: ship-route raider.
- Guard: land-route anti-raider, attacks highwaymen.
- Bounty Hunter: ship-route anti-raider, attacks pirates and can extract bounty gold.
- Worker: works in factories; productivity tied to meld count.
- Bum: low-activity profession that can find leftover things in the home city.
- Players used decoy vehicle strategies. An Ars snippet calls one strategy "bubbling": send empty vehicles before and after the cargo vehicle so attackers get tied up fighting decoys while cargo passes.

## UI Evidence From Screenshots

- Header shows player name, level in parentheses, gold, credits, battery time, and log out button.
- Top nav: current city, Home, Miners, Mines, Cities, Shop, Forums, Facebook, Help.
- Left nav: Things, Findings, Gadgets, Ships, Factories, Profession, Messages, Account.
- Home page: central discovery feed plus right-side Discoveries/Listings/Bids columns.
- Findings page: mine cards with robot/mine art, mine type, mine number, mine gold/mine things radio buttons, bucket rate, right-side findings queue, and "Email upon next finding."
- Shop page: purchase/rent mines with credits, gold market links, battery extension, inventory expansion.
- Profession page: table of profession descriptions and "Become a ..." buttons.
- Things/profile page: city inventory, global inventory count, filter button, meld list, "Load more melds."
- Mine type browse page: categories for Melds, Routes, and Boosts; item list with colored icons and weights in parentheses.
- Item detail page: item name, mine type, advertise link, Watch This button, description, inventory count, meld requirement, item image, Buy/Sell/Trash, Listings/Bids/Sales/Foreign Listings.
