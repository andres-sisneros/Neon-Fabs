# Neon Fabs Lore Bible

## Tone

Neon Fabs is about contract drifters, civic matter, sleeper crews, and machine-made scarcity. The world should feel cyberpunk, but not only chrome and violence: public assemblers, route debt, soft borders, and fragile communities should matter.

## Core Premise

Cities are stitched together by old routes and newer fab networks. Matter printers can make almost anything, but access is rationed through contracts, civic permissions, market listings, and transport risk. Players begin as small operators with one starter fab, turning overlooked street relics into melds that increase their operating time.

## Influences To Internalize

- Cyberpunk Red: street-level hustle, roles, cargo risk, security contracts, and city markets.
- The Diamond Age: matter compilers, civic order, artificial scarcity, education-by-object, and societies shaped by who controls fabrication.
- Citizen Sleeper / Citizen Sleeper 2: precarious crews, orbital-worker debt, fragile bodies, faction favors, and survival through networks rather than heroics.
- Death Stranding: delivery as social infrastructure, dangerous corridors, shared route maintenance, and the communal relief of making a road safer for everyone.

## World Rules

- Fabs are not magic. They are local machines constrained by feedstock, permissions, maintenance, and power.
- Markets are local because inventory is local. Buying in a city stores the item there.
- Vehicles are printed route tools. They move cargo, but later they can be delayed, damaged, stolen, or restricted by route type.
- Melds are civic-memory sets: fused patterns of objects that stabilize the player’s operating identity and increase battery capacity.
- Melds are not normal inventory. To move home cities later, a player must deconstruct melds, move the components through route risk, and fuse them again in the new home city.
- Routes remember labor. Clearing hostile crews, maintaining corridors, and moving goods can temporarily lower route pressure for everyone using that connection.
- Scarcity is political. The world can print abundance, but access is always mediated by someone.
- Starter accounts own only the Starter Fab. Additional fabs can be bought or rented later, with a hard cap of three active fabs.

## Naming Guidelines

- Common objects should sound street-level and worn: token, badge, patch, card, skiff, kiosk.
- Rare objects should suggest institutional weirdness: charter, civic, ghost, redacted, founder.
- Avoid direct borrowed terms or names from inspirations. Use the mood, not the nouns.
